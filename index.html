About Me!

Connor Perez is a currently participating in Northwestern's Full Stack Coding Bootcamp. He was born and raised in Chicago, and graduted from Tulane University with a major in Latin American Studies, and minors in Business and Spanish. After completing his major and minor requirements, Connor took coding classes for fun his senior year at Tulane (one of which was taught in Spanish), where he was first introduced to computer science.

After graduating, Connor worked in the public health field, most notably for the organization Partners In Health in Boston. At Partners In Health, he worked in donor support and fundraising, while taking on additional responsibilities in Dev Ops, managing data and completing large data imports and exports. After moving back home, Connor worked on quantatative analysis research for clinical drug trials before deciding to change careers and join the Northwestern program.

In his free time, Connor enjoys cooking, eating, travelling, attending Cubs games, and reading fantasy or science fiction novels. He is specifically interested in food chemistry as well. At any point in time, it is likely that there are vegetables and a home made beer fermenting in his dark kitchen pantry. Connor is also a lover of animals, and with his fiancé, owns 2 cats - Jacque'mo and Forrest.
